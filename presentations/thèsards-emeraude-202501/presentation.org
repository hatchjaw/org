** Sample-synchronous (almost) networked audio

Aims:
#+ATTR_REVEAL: :frag (appear)
- Establish an authoritative source of *time* and *audio data*.
- Transmit time from the *clock authority* to any/all *clock subscribers*
  via *Precision Time Protocol* (PTP).
- Transmit audio data from the *audio server* to any/all *audio
  clients* via multicast *User Datagram Protocol* (UDP).
- The *clock authority* is (almost certainly) also the *audio server*.

#+REVEAL: split

Challenges (non-exhaustive list):
#+ATTR_REVEAL: :frag (appear)
- Use PTP time correction to *condition the audio subsystem clock* of
  connected clients.
- Ensure that both the *rates* and *interrupt timing* of the client
  audio systems are synchronised.
  #+ATTR_REVEAL: :frag (appear)
  + If rates are matched, but signal reproduction is offset, the
    result is a *filter*.

*** Test setup

#+ATTR_REVEAL: :frag (appear)
- Four Teensies
  #+ATTR_REVEAL: :frag (appear)
  + One audio server --- transmits a 1 Hz impulse train to the
    network.
    #+ATTR_REVEAL: :frag (appear)
    * Also acts as a /clock authority/ --- transmits PTP /sync/ messages
      to the network and responds to /delay request/ messages.
  + Three audio clients --- respond to PTP /sync/ messages; send
    /delay request/ messages.
    #+ATTR_REVEAL: :frag (appear)
    * Also receive, buffer and reproduce the impulse train audio
      signal.
- Packets are timestamped
  + Clients reproduce the packet with timestamp closest to their
    current ethernet clock time.

*** Takeaways

#+ATTR_REVEAL: :frag (appear)
- The audio clock correction algorithm reduces audio clock drift from
  ~6 ppm to *< 2 ppb*
  #+ATTR_REVEAL: :frag (appear)
  + 6 \micro{}s per sec \to 2 ns per sec.
  + Context: 1 sample period @ 48 kHz \approx 20 \micro{}s.
  + More context: AES guideline is 5% of the reference frame (1 \micro{}s @
    48 kHz).
- The remaining drift appears to be inversely correlated with the raw
  drift.
- Startup/interrupt synchronicity remains a challenge.
- There's an uncomfortable reliance on the cheapest PTP-compliant
  switch on the market.
