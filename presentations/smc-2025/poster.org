#+latex: \justifying

Spatial audio has an *accessibility* problem, but perhaps we can improve
the situation by *distributing* the signal processing. Wave field
synthesis, for example, parallelises quite nicely\nbsp[cite:@bellochPerformance2021].

#+latex: \vspace{.5cm}\begin{center}\scalebox{2.1}{
\begin{tikzpicture}[font=\ultratiny]
  \pic {centralised};
  \node [single arrow, fill=red!50!black, inner ysep=3mm] at (9.75, 0);
  \pic at (11.5, 0) {distributed};
\end{tikzpicture}
#+latex: }\end{center}

**** Left
:PROPERTIES:
:BEAMER_col: 0.53
:BEAMER_opt: [T]
:END:

#+latex: \justifying

Distributing things in this manner requires that our group of signal
processors is *synchronised*, however, otherwise the resulting
soundfield will be destroyed.  A 1\nbsp{}ms asynchronicity between two
distributed audio devices would result in a *propagation discrepancy* of
over 30\nbsp{}cm.

*** Asynchronicity                                                  :B_block:
:PROPERTIES:
:BEAMER_env: block
:END:

#+latex: \justifying

We can configure a network of audio-capable devices (Teensy 4.1
microcontroller development boards) to run at a given target sampling
rate, but if we had a way of measuring /true/ time − and thus true
frequency − we would find that they're actually running *slightly
faster* or *slightly slower* than instructed.

#+latex: \vspace{.5cm}\begin{center}\scalebox{2.25}{
\begin{tikzpicture}[font=\supertiny]
  \pic at (0, 0) {teensythinks={nominalfs=48, actualfs=47.998}};
  \pic at (6, 0) {teensythinks={nominalfs=48, actualfs=48.001}};
  \pic at (12, 0) {teensythinks={nominalfs=48, actualfs=47.999}};
\end{tikzpicture}
#+latex: }\end{center}

Each device has its own *distinct internal time reference* − a crystal
oscillator (XO) − and each one of those time references behaves
slightly differently. XOs are rated to a given tolerance − typically
reported in /parts per million/ (ppm) − an amount by which they may be
expected to deviate from their nominal frequency.

*** Time Exchange

**** Left
:PROPERTIES:
:BEAMER_col: 0.58
:BEAMER_opt: [T]
:END:

#+latex: \justifying

IEEE 1588 *Precision Time Protocol* (PTP) is a nanosecond-order
time-exchange mechanism. Clock authority and subscriber(s) exchange
per-second timestamped packets until the subscriber has enough
information to determine its *drift* and *offset* relative to the
authority. These values are typically fed to a proportional integral
controller, and the result used as a /nanoseconds-per-second/
adjustment value to be applied to a dedicated PTP timer.

#+latex: \begin{center}\scalebox{2.3}{
\begin{tikzpicture}[font=\supertiny]
  \node [] at (0, 0) {
    \includegraphics[width=7cm]{./images/switch}
  };
  \node [draw=red!50!black, ultra thick, star, star points=10, rotate=15, fill=yellow, inner sep=.1mm] at (2, .75) {€ 200};
\end{tikzpicture}
#+latex: }\end{center}

Consumer-grade networking equipment does not tend to support PTP, but
relatively inexpensive exceptions do exist. What's more, our chosen
microcontroller platform supports it too\nbsp[cite:@schleusnerSubMicrosecond2024].

**** Right
:PROPERTIES:
:BEAMER_col: 0.38
:BEAMER_opt: [T]
:END:

#+latex: \begin{center}\scalebox{2.3}{
\begin{tikzpicture}[font=\supertiny]
  \pic {ptp};
\end{tikzpicture}
#+latex: }\end{center}

\begin{gather*}
\text{Drift} = 1 - \frac{t'_{2}-t_{2}}{t'_{1}-t_{1}}\\[10pt]
\text{Delay} = \frac{(t_{4} - t_{1}) - (t_{3} - t_{2}) }{2}\\[10pt]
\text{Offset} = t_{2} - t_{1} - \text{Delay}
\end{gather*}

*** Media Clock Recovery                                            :B_block:
:PROPERTIES:
:BEAMER_env: block
:END:

#+latex: \justifying

A target sampling rate \(\hat{F}_s\) for the Teensy is found as
\begin{equation}
\hat{F}_{s} = \frac{F_{XO}}{2^{8}}\frac{D_{S}+\frac{D_{N}}{D_{D}}}{D_{p}D_{A}D_{I_{1}}D_{I_{2}}}
\end{equation}
\(F_{XO}\) is the device's 24 MHz crystal oscillator, and the various
\(D\) parameters are hardware *clock divider registers*. \(D_{N}\), the
audio PLL fractional numerator register, provides *30 bits of
resolution*; more than enough to effect nanosecond-order
adjustments\nbsp[cite:@carotGNSSbased2019]. Rearranging:
#+name: eq:dn
\begin{equation}
D_{N} = D_{D}\left(\frac{2^{8}\hat{F}_{s}D_{p}D_{A}D_{I_{1}}D_{I_{2}}}{F_{XO}} - D_{S}\right)
\end{equation}
Applying a PTP-derived adjustment (in seconds) to the nominal sampling
rate gives us a figure that can be plugged into eqref:eq:dn
\begin{equation}
\hat{F}_{s} = F_{s}(1 + \text{Adjust})
\end{equation}

#+latex: \justifying

Conditioning the media clock alone is not enough. To guarantee that
samples will be *presented simultaneously*, we wait until a sub-100 ns
PTP lock is achieved (takes about ten seconds) to start clock
subscriber audio subsystems. This entails starting presentation time
on a given PTP second, so it's easiest when working with a nominal
sampling rate that's a multiple of the buffer size!
\begin{equation*}
\frac{44\,100}{128} = 344.53125 \quad\quad\quad
\frac{48\,000}{128} = 375
\end{equation*}

using CSV, DataFrames, Plots, LaTeXStrings

function plotDrift(dataCategory)
    scalefontsizes(2)
    t41lastBytes = [191, 203, 234]
    maxOffset = 0
    yMax = 0
    plot(fontfamily="Minion Pro Regular",
         size=(900, 645),
         bottom_margin=5Plots.mm,
         left_margin=5Plots.mm,
         right_margin=5Plots.mm,
         top_margin=7Plots.mm,
         xlabel="Time (s)",
         xtickfontfamily="Computer Modern",
         ytickfontfamily="Computer Modern",
         legendtitle="IP address",
         legendfont="iosevka-comfy-motion-normalregularupright",
         legendfontsize=22,
         legendtitlefontsize=22,
         tickfontsize=22,
         guidefontsize=26,
         gridlinewidth=2);
    for b in t41lastBytes
        local data = CSV.read("../../papers/smc-2025/data/$b-$dataCategory.csv", DataFrame; header=["offset"], limit=600)
        data.offset .= data.offset .- data.offset[1]
        # Prevent `noptp` data for client 191 from wrapping.
        if b == 191 && dataCategory == "noptp"
            data.offset[391:end] .= data.offset[391:end] .+ (data.offset[390] - data.offset[391])
        end
        plot!(data.offset, label="x.x.48.$b", linewidth=3)

        # Get order-of-magnitude info
        max = maximum(abs.(data.offset))
        if max > maxOffset
            maxOffset = max
        end
        max = maximum(data.offset)
        if max > yMax
            yMax = max
        end
    end

    ordOfMag = floor(Int, log10(maxOffset))
    ordOffset = 8e-8
    if dataCategory == "noptp"
        ordOffset = 3.5e-4
        plot!(ylabel="Offset (s)")
        plot!(title="Raw drift")
    else
        plot!(title="Conditioned clocks")
    end
    annotate!(12, yMax + ordOffset, (latexstring("\$\\times10^{$(ordOfMag)}\$"), 22))
    plot!(yformatter=y -> string(y / 10.0^ordOfMag))

    savefig("./images/gen/$(dataCategory)_poster.png")
    scalefontsizes()
end

#+latex: \justifying

Without sampling rate conditioning, a trio of clock subscribers,
outputting a pulse-per-second test audio signal, drift on the order of
7 ppm; more than 1.5 m propagation discrepancy after ten minutes. With
PTP-based correction, *drift is reduced to less than 2 parts per
billion* and a propagation discrepancy of less than 1 mm.
