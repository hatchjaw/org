#+title:      Differentiation: The Chain Rule
#+date:       [2024-08-19 lun. 09:50]
#+filetags:   :autodiff:maths:
#+identifier: 20240819T095042

Let \(y = f(\mathbf{v})\) be a differentiable function of \(m\)
independent variables; for each \(j \in 1,\dots,m\), let \(v_{j} =
v_{j}(\mathbf{x})\) be a differentiable function of \(n\) independent
variables:

#+NAME: eq:diff-functions
\begin{align}
y &= f(\mathbf{v}) = f(v_{1}, v_{2}, \dots, v_{m}), \\
v_{j} &= v_{j}(\mathbf{x}) = v_{j}(x_{1}, x_{2}, \dots, x_{n}),
\end{align}

then:

#+NAME: eq:chain-rule
\begin{equation}
\frac{\partial{}y}{\partial{}x_{i}} &= \sum_{j=1}^{m}\frac{\partial{}y}{\partial{}v_{j}}\frac{\partial{}v_{j}}{\partial{}x_{x}}
\end{equation}

for any \(i \in 1,\dots,n\).

To give an audio-relevant example, consider a gain control, \(x_{1}\), and
a DC offset, \(x_{2}\), applied to an input signal, \(s_{in}\):

#+NAME: eq:chainrule-example
\begin{align}
y &= f(\mathbf{v}) = f(v_{1}, v_{2}, v_{3}) = v_{1}(\mathbf{x})v_{2}(\mathbf{x}) + v_{3}(\mathbf{x}) \\
 & v_{1}(\mathbf{x}) = s_{in},\, v_{2}(\mathbf{x}) = x_{1},\, v_{3}(\mathbf{x}) = x_{2} \\
\frac{\partial{}y}{\partial{}x_{i}} &= \sum_{j=1}^{3}\frac{\partial{}y}{\partial{}v_{1}}\frac{\partial{}v_{1}}{\partial{}x_{i}} \\
  &= \frac{\partial{}y}{\partial{}v_{1}}\frac{\partial{}v_{1}}{\partial{}x_{i}} + \frac{\partial{}y}{\partial{}v_{2}}\frac{\partial{}v_{2}}{\partial{}x_{i}} + \frac{\partial{}y}{\partial{}v_{3}}\frac{\partial{}v_{3}}{\partial{}x_{i}} \\
i = 1, &= v_{2}\cdot{}0 + v_{1}\cdot{}1 + 1\cdot{}0 \\
  &= v_{1} \\
  &= s_{in}, \\
i = 2, &= v_{2}\cdot{}0 + v_{1}\cdot{}0 + 1\cdot1 \\
  &= 1
\end{align}
